I’m currently working on the website for the Meanwood Kwamwena Community Hub — a community-driven project close to my heart.

Until now, there hasn’t been a public, centralized way to keep up with what’s happening in the community. This project is my attempt to address that gap.

The main focus is to deploy it as a minimal news platform. I’m taking a no-fuss, straight-to-the-point approach. The goal is to make the site highly scannable, so readers can quickly find stories, updates, and important information without distractions. No gimmicks — just clear, useful content that’s easy to access and easy to navigate.

The site will be enhanced gently, with features being rolled out gradually as it evolves. These improvements will be introduced thoughtfully over time, ensuring the platform remains simple, reliable, and easy to use while growing in capability.

My priority is ensuring that the content remains up to date, accurate, and relevant. I’ve put together a practical content plan to keep things moving, covering stakeholder engagement, content sourcing, and publishing.

I’m aiming to complete the first phase shortly.

Thanks for taking the time to read.
