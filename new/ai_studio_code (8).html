Phase 3 Drainage System Construction Officially Underway

Meanwood Kwamwena — Residents of Kwamwena Phase 3 can finally breathe a sigh of relief as the long-awaited drainage construction project officially kicked off this week. The project, aimed at mitigating the perennial flooding issues experienced during the rainy season, marks a significant milestone in the development of our valley.

Contractors from BuildWell Zambia mobilized on-site early Monday morning, beginning with the grading of the main access routes and the excavation of primary drainage channels along Great East Extension Road.

Addressing Seasonal Flooding

For years, residents in the lower sections of Phase 3 have faced challenges with waterlogging, often making roads impassable during heavy downpours. The new system is designed to channel water efficiently towards the main Chongwe river tributaries, bypassing residential zones.

"This is not just about digging trenches; it's about securing our homes and improving the value of our properties. We are grateful that the Council and the Developers have finally acted on our petitions."
— Mr. Phiri, Phase 3 Resident Representative

Project Timeline and Scope

The engineering team has outlined a comprehensive 4-month timeline to ensure major works are completed before the peak of the rainy season in January. The scope of work includes:

  • Excavation of 5km of primary storm drains.
  • Installation of concrete culverts at 12 key intersections.
  • Reinforcement of bridge crossings near the market area.
  • Grading and compacting of adjacent service roads.

Traffic Diversions

Residents are advised that there will be temporary road closures and diversions in place. The main entrance to Phase 3 via the Police Post will remain open, but the shortcut route through Section D will be closed for the next three weeks.

We urge all motorists to drive with caution, observe the temporary signage, and cooperate with the flagmen on site. The Hub will continue to provide weekly updates on the progress of the construction.
